Turning Points

My UW experience has been much more than an academic experience; I can confidently say that I grew much more as a person, than I grew as a student. The person I am today is almost unrecognizable from the person that I was 4 years ago as a high school senior. I now know what gives me energy and what type of work I naturally gravitate towards; I understand my passions.

In the following section are significant turning points in the past few years that have shaped me to who I am today.

2018-2019 (Freshman year)

In the past, as a high school senior, I was narrow-minded. I was hyper focused on reaching financial success that I could achieve by pursuing STEM and getting a high salary. Things like art, poetry and ethnic studies are some of the things that I felt were useless distractors without much of a ‘real’ purpose. A lot of these sentiments turned on its head within weeks of stepping onto campus, where I found myself spending hours on work I would’ve never expected myself doing.

  • November 2018

    Dylan Tran & Southeast Asian Recruiter

    My high school friend Yuhaniz invited me to tag along to a random meeting where I met Dylan Tran (Husky 100 in 2018!) who was advocating VP Rickey Hall to hire a Southeast Asian student recruiter. This meeting wasn’t through any official university channels, but was initiated by Dylan himself. I was amazed at his tenacity and passion for change; changing the course of my next two years.

  • January 2019

    Rising SEAs Delegation

    Working with Dylan, Yuhaniz, and new friends, we created a new RSO called Rising Southeast Asians (SEAs) Delegation aimed to uplift Southeast Asian high school and university students. We also learned Rickey Hall heeded our concerns and dedicated funding for a SEA recruiter!

  • February 2019

    Rejected from Costco HQ Internship

    I was really interested in exploring business, but I was rejected because I needed more experience ‘talking and working with others’. This feedback hurt a lot but I took it in stride.

  • May 2019

    SEA Unity Kickback

    Organizing this event and witnessing people building new connections and learning new things was super rewarding and made me want to find more opportunities!

  • Summer 2019

    Started working at Avanti Sports

    After the Costco rejection, I took a step completely outside my comfort zone and started working at a local tennis shop as a salesperson! This job gave business and social skills that helped me with future community organizing work!

2019-2020 (Sophomore year)

In freshman year, it wasn’t unusual for me to spend upwards to 20 hours a week in meetings related to Rising SEAs Delegation. This work didn’t feel like work; this type of work gave me energy and I loved it! My previous priorities of engineering and career were long gone; I had only spent a few hours writing my application for the Computer Engineering major before submitting!

  • August 2019

    Acceptance into Computer Engineering

    This acceptance gave me a breath of fresh air, security, and comfort that allowed me to focus for the rest of the year on things that I really cared about.

  • October 2019

    Becoming a KhSA Officer

    Yuhaniz, the same friend that introduced me to Dylan, also introduced me to the Khmer Student Association! My first KhSA meeting was a liberating experience and made me feel like I actually had a home on campus. I applied for an officer position and got it!

  • January 2020

    KhSA NW Conference

    One of my first tasks was planning for the Northwest Conference, a huge annual conference for KhSAs around the country to come together. We had 100+ participants, had amazing meaningful workshops that left people in tears, and we built new relationships that still last today.

  • January 2020

    Dropped Physics 121 & Changed Majors

    Long story short, I'd skipped too many physics lectures to plan for the conference, so I had to drop the class and switch my major to Computer Science. This gave me the freedom to take more ethnic studies classes and focus on things I really cared about!

  • May 2020

    KhSA Scholarship

    One of the most rewarding tasks was leading our high school scholarship program. It was a very humbling position, as I was in charge of crafting essay questions, selection criteria, and a committee for Cambodian students, even though I wasn’t Cambodian myself.

  • Summer 2020

    PPIA Fellowship

    Acceptance into the highly selective Public Policy & International Affairs fellowship program at Carnegie Mellon University was affirmation that others saw my potential for public service. I was the only student from a technical background which was intimidating, and I had never done work like this before. My successful completion of this fellowship affirmed to myself that I had the capabilities to pursue graduate school and become a public servant.

2020-2021 (Junior year)

My sophomore year was full of fun and excitement, along with a lot of rewarding community work. But heading deep into the COVID-19 pandemic, I decided that I wanted to ‘settle down’ and focus on my career. That meant seeking an engineering internship, finally focusing on my schoolwork, and giving myself a break from community work.

  • October 2020

    Seattle Community Network

    This idea of taking a break from community work quickly took a turn when I stumbled upon Seattle Community Network. I realized that I could focus on Computer Science while still giving back to the community! I took this opportunity and ran with it. For the entire next year of the pandemic, I spent 20 hours a week doing research with the CS department, connecting with community organizations, setting up free Internet, and doing engineering work that had a social impact!

  • July 2021

    Accepted into CSE BS/MS

    With the Seattle Community Network, I realized that I could pursue community work and engineering at the same time! I applied and was accepted to the master’s program so that I could spend more time researching the intersection of computer science and social good!

  • Summer 2021

    F5 Software Engineering Internship

What Now?

The past few years have been really rewarding but have also been a ton of work that has taken a toll on me. The pandemic has been hitting me, my family, and my friends hard, and I have a lot of years left in my life. To preserve myself I’m taking things slow and focusing on one thing at a time. I’m investing in myself and setting up for long term successes.

  • Autumn 2021

    American Ethnic Studies Major

    If I want to become a public servant in the future, it's important I have a holistic understanding of the backgrounds of those I’d like to serve. I am now pursuing a double degree in American Ethnic Studies and Computer Science with a minor in Diversity!

  • Autumn 2021

    CSE 351 Teaching Assistant

    I have a strong interest in becoming a teacher one day. As a teaching assistant in the Allen School I had gained a lot of experience and learned that I might be able to!

  • June 2022

    Graduate with American Ethnic Studies and Computer Science degree!

  • Summer 2022

    F5 Software Engineering Internship

  • Autumn 2022

    Final Year of School!

  • June 2023

    Graduate with M.S. in Computer Science

  • Future

    Engineering? Community Work? Teaching? PhD? M.S. in Public Policy?

    Still not sure what I want to do and I’m okay with it! I’m looking forward to what the future has for me.
