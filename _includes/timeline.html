Turning Points

My UW experience has been much more than an academic experience. I can confidently say that I grew much more as a person, than I did as a student. UW has connected me to endless opportunities and communities, enabling me to discover my strengths, understand my passions, and build a strong network of people I trust. The following are significant points in the past few years that have shaped me to who I am today.

2018-2019 (Freshman year)

Entering freshman year, I was narrow-minded and preoccupied with achieving financial success, and college would be my tool to reach that. Art, ethnic studies, and activism were examples of areas I felt weren’t ‘practical’ or ‘useful’. These sentiments turned on its head soon after stepping onto campus, where I found myself spending hours on work I would’ve never expected myself doing.

  • November 2018

    Dylan Tran & Southeast Asian Recruiter

    I met Dylan Tran (Husky 100 in 2018!) at a random meeting advocating VP Rickey Hall to hire a Southeast Asian student recruiter. This meeting was initiated by Dylan himself, outside of official university channels. I was amazed at his tenacity and he taught me the power that youth could have to enact change.

  • January 2019

    Rising SEAs Delegation

    With Dylan and new friends, we created an RSO called Rising Southeast Asians (SEAs) Delegation to uplift SEA high school and university students. We also learned Rickey Hall heeded our concerns and dedicated funding for a SEA recruiter!

  • February 2019

    Rejected from Costco Business Internship

    I was really excited about exploring business, but was rejected because I needed more experience ‘talking and working with others’. This was my first major failure and the feedback was demoralizing.

  • May 2019

    Rising SEAs Unity Kickback

    Seeing my hard work come to fruition was a magical experience. I ran into the event an hour late in awe, watching the crowds of SEAs mingling and building connections that they wouldn’t have otherwise. This was a turning point that made me want to seek further opportunities to foster community.

  • Summer 2019

    Started Working at Avanti Sports

    After the Costco rejection, I sought to improve my social skills. I started working at a local tennis shop as a salesperson which helped prepare me for future community organizing work!

2019-2020 (Sophomore year)

In freshman year, I dedicated a lot of time to Rising SEAs Delegation but it didn’t feel like work; this type of work gave me energy. My previous priorities of engineering and career were long gone. I wanted to seek more opportunities like this where I’d be able to have a bigger impact.

  • August 2019

    Acceptance into Computer Engineering

    This gave me a breath of fresh air and security that allowed me to focus the rest of my time on what I really cared about.

  • October 2019

    Becoming a KhSA Officer

    Through the Khmer Student Association (KhSA), I found a home on campus. I applied for an officer position because I felt like the skills I got from Rising SEAs Delegation and Avanti Sports would make me an asset.

  • January 2020

    KhSA NW Conference

    As a new officer, newcomer into KhSA, and a non-Khmer person, I felt a lot of pressure to prove my worth. My first task was planning this huge annual conference for KhSAs around the country to come together. Witnessing this come to fruition was another magical experience as I watched people build meaningful relationships that still last today.

  • January 2020

    Dropped Physics 121 & Changed Majors

    Long story short, I'd skipped all my physics lectures to plan for the conference, so I had to drop physics. As a result, I switched my major to Computer Science and added a minor in Diversity. This gave me the freedom to take more ethnic studies classes and focus on things I really cared about!

  • May 2020

    KhSA Scholarship

    One of the most rewarding tasks was leading our high school scholarship program. It was a very humbling position, as I was in charge of crafting essay questions, selection criteria, and a committee for Cambodian students, even though I wasn’t Cambodian myself.

  • Summer 2020

    PPIA Fellowship

    Acceptance into the highly selective Public Policy & International Affairs fellowship program at Carnegie Mellon University was affirmation that others saw my potential for public service. I was intimidated as a STEM student who’d never done work like this before. But at the end of the fellowship, my peers came together to surprise me with their own custom award of appreciation! This experience proved to myself that I was in the right place.

2020-2021 (Junior year)

My sophomore year was full of excitement and intense, rewarding community work. But heading deep into the COVID-19 pandemic, I decided that I wanted to ‘settle down’ and focus on my career. That meant seeking an internship, focusing on school, and taking a break from extracurriculars.

  • October 2020

    Seattle Community Network

    This ‘taking a break’ quickly took a turn when I stumbled upon Seattle Community Network. I realized I could do engineering while still giving back to the community! I took this opportunity and ran with it; for the next year, I spent countless hours doing research with the CS department, connecting with community organizations, setting up free Internet, and making a social impact!

  • July 2021

    Accepted into CSE 5th Year Master’s Program

    With the Seattle Community Network, I realized that I could pursue community work and engineering at the same time! This enables me to further explore the intersection of computer science and social good!

  • Summer 2021

    F5 Software Engineering Internship

What Now?

The past few years have been really rewarding but the workload has taken a toll on me. The pandemic has been hitting me and my family really hard. To preserve myself I’m finally taking things slow, setting myself up for long term success.

  • Autumn 2021

    American Ethnic Studies Major

    I knew that I'd like to serve marginalized communities in some form and having a holistic understanding of the backgrounds of those I’d like to serve is important to me. I am now pursuing a double degree in American Ethnic Studies and Computer Science!

  • Autumn 2021

    CSE 351 Teaching Assistant

    As a person with strong interest in becoming a teacher one day, this gave me a glimpse into what it’d be like!

  • June 2022

    Graduate with American Ethnic Studies and Computer Science degree!

  • Summer 2022

    F5 Software Engineering Internship

  • Autumn 2022

    Final Year of School!

  • June 2023

    Graduate with M.S. in Computer Science!

  • Future

    Engineering? Community Work? Public Policy? Teaching? PhD?

    These past years at UW haven’t been part of some grand, carefully calculated plan. It's been a combination of lots of luck, learning from past experiences, and following my passions. I am now more than confident that with any future hurdle that comes at me, I will be able to adapt and take it in stride. Although I still don’t know what I want for my future, I know that I can trust myself to do what's right for me.
